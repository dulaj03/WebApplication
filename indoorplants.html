Monstera

The Monstera plant, often called the Swiss Cheese Plant, is a popular tropical houseplant known for its large, glossy, and uniquely perforated leaves. Native to Central and South America, it thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect sunlight. Monstera is easy to care for, requiring moderate watering and occasional pruning. Its striking foliage makes it a favorite for home decor, adding a lush, jungle-like feel to any space.

Snake Plant

Light: Tolerates low light, but prefers bright, indirect light. Water: Water every 2–3 weeks. Allow the soil to dry out between waterings. Soil: Well-draining potting mix (cactus/succulent mix is ideal). Tips: Avoid overwatering; it’s drought-tolerant. Wipe leaves to keep dust-free.

Peace Lily

Light: Prefers bright, indirect light but tolerates low light. Water: Water once a week; keep the soil consistently moist but not soggy. Soil: Rich, well-draining potting soil. Tips: Wilts slightly when thirsty. Clean leaves and mist occasionally.

Aloe Vera

Light: Needs bright, indirect sunlight or some direct sun. Water: Water deeply but infrequently—every 2–3 weeks. Soil: Well-draining cactus/succulent mix. Tips: Use a pot with drainage holes. Don’t let water sit in the rosette.

Pothos

Light: Thrives in low to bright, indirect light. Water: Water every 1–2 weeks; let soil dry between waterings. Soil: Standard potting mix. Tips: Easy to propagate in water. Trim vines to control length and promote fullness.

Spider Plant

Light: Prefers bright, indirect light; tolerates low light. Water: Water weekly, allowing the soil to dry out between waterings. Soil: Well-draining general potting mix. Tips: Produces "babies" that can be propagated. Avoid fluoride in water if tips brown.

ZZ Plant

Light: Low to bright, indirect light. Water: Water every 2–3 weeks; very drought-tolerant. Soil: Well-draining potting soil. Tips: Avoid overwatering. Shiny leaves can be wiped clean occasionally.

Rubber Plant

Light: Bright, indirect light. Water: Water every 1–2 weeks. Keep soil slightly moist. Soil: Well-draining potting soil with peat or compost. Tips: Wipe leaves to remove dust. Rotate plant for even growth.
